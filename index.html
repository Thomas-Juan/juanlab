Cardiovascular development · Genetic tools · Zebrafish genetics

Blood flow forces shape the cardiovascular system.

The lab studies how hemodynamic forces control cardiovascular development and function. We build genetic tools to control cardiac contractions and probe mechanosensor function in endothelial and cardiovascular cell populations.

Endothelium Mechanotransduction

Blood flow mechanosensation in endothelial cells

Endothelial cells are in direct contact with blood flow and provide a first-line response to hemodynamic forces. The lab investigates the mechanosensitive proteins and pathways that convert these physical cues into developmental decisions.

Zebrafish Live imaging

Zebrafish as a window into cardiovascular development

Zebrafish embryos allow rapid genetics, optical access, and in vivo imaging of cardiac and vascular morphogenesis. Their transparency and resilience make them an ideal system for studying force-dependent developmental processes.

CRISPR Conditional knockdown

Genetic tools to interrogate cardiovascular gene function

The lab develops conditional loss-of-function approaches to control cardiac contraction and bypass limits of classical mutants, including compensation and protein persistence.
